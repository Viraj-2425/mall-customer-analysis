Mall Customer Data Analysis
Project Overview

This project performs Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) on the Mall Customers dataset to understand customer demographics, income patterns, spending behavior, and business insights. The analysis uses statistical methods and visualizations to identify trends that can help businesses improve customer targeting and marketing strategies.

Dataset Information

The dataset contains information about 200 mall customers with the following features:

CustomerID
Gender
Age
Annual Income (k$)
Spending Score (1-100)
Objectives
Analyze customer demographics and spending behavior.
Identify patterns in customer income and spending.
Compare spending habits across genders.
Perform correlation analysis between customer attributes.
Calculate confidence intervals for spending scores.
Generate business recommendations based on findings.

Key Findings
No missing values were found in the dataset.
Annual Income showed the highest variability among all features.
Female customers had a slightly higher average spending score than male customers.
Annual Income had almost no correlation with Spending Score.
Younger customers tended to spend more than older customers.
The estimated average spending score falls within a 95% confidence interval of approximately 46.60 to 53.80.
Business Recommendations
Focus marketing efforts on high-spending customers.
Develop personalized campaigns for different customer segments.
Offer loyalty programs to premium customers.
Target younger customers through digital marketing channels.
Create engagement strategies for high-income customers with low spending behavior.
Conclusion

This project demonstrates the use of data analysis techniques to extract meaningful insights from customer data. The findings can help businesses understand customer behavior, improve marketing strategies, and make data-driven decisions.
